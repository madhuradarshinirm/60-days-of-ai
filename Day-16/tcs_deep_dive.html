Strengths

  • Virtually debt-free balance sheet with exceptional ROE (~52%) and ROCE (~63%), among the best in Indian large-cap IT
  • Strong and consistently rising free cash flow (₹48,000+ Cr in FY26)
  • High, sustained dividend payout (~81% in FY26) and attractive current yield (~3.23%)

Watch-points

  • Revenue growth has decelerated meaningfully — 5Y CAGR of 10% down to TTM of ~5%, flagged as "poor" by automated screens
  • FII holding has declined steadily over the past two years, even as DIIs have absorbed the difference

Key metric to track

Quarterly revenue growth (constant currency) and deal TCV (total contract value) commentary in upcoming earnings calls — these will signal whether the demand slowdown is bottoming out. Next results date: 9 July 2026 (Q1 FY27).

Overall fundamental quality

Strong — TCS's balance sheet strength and capital efficiency are best-in-class; the main uncertainty is top-line growth reacceleration, not financial health.
