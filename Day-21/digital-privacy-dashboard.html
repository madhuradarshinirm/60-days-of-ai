Final Verdict

Significant footprint, fair privacy posture — concentrated mainly in one ecosystem.

Across the 15 reported services, exposure is Significant (74/100), driven by dense overlap between social, entertainment, gaming, shopping and financial activity. Privacy posture is Fair (41/100) — the use of end‑to‑end encrypted messengers (WhatsApp, iMessage) helps, but broad reliance on ad-supported, engagement-driven platforms pulls the score down.

Estimated ecosystem concentration sits at 27/100, led by Google/Alphabet's four services (Search, YouTube, Pay, Photos) — this is the single highest-leverage point for cross-service correlation, followed by Meta's two services (Instagram, WhatsApp).

The three highest-leverage actions, in order: (1) tighten Google account & ad personalization settings, (2) reduce location sharing on Snapchat/TikTok, (3) audit Google Pay's connected merchants and apps.

All figures above are Estimates derived from the reported app list and general, publicly known platform behavior. This report does not access, store, or query any private or third-party database, and does not claim certainty about any individual trait, identity, or behavior.
